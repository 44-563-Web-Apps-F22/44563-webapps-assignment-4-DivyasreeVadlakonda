MEN

Well, Sergeant Meryll, and how fares thy pretty charge,Elsie Maynard?

WOMEN

Well enough, sir. She is quite strong again, and leaves us to-night.

MEN

Thanks to Dame Carruthers' kind nursing, eh?

[Exeunt all but CHORUS, who remains weeping]

CHORUS

Nay, Sergeant Meryll, don't go. I have something of grave import to say to thee.

WOMEN

It's coming.

MEN

[laughing] I'faith, I think I', not wanted here.

[Going]

[Men raise CHORUS, and carry him off on their shoulders.]

CHORUS

Nay, Master Leonard, I've naught to say to thy father that his son may not hear.

MEN

True. I'm one of the family; I had forgotten!

CHORUS

Tis about this Elsie Maynard. A pretty girl, Master Leonard.

MEN

Aye, fair as a peach blossom— what then?

CHORUS

She hath a liking for thee, or I mistake not.

MEN

With all my heart. She's as dainty a little amid as you'll find in a midsummer day's march.

CHORUS

Then be warned in time, and give not thy heart to her. Oh, I know what it is to give my heart to one who will have none of it!

WOMEN

[aside] Aye, she knows all about that. [Aloud] And why is my boy to take heed of her? She's a good girl, Dame Carruthers.

CHORUS

Good enough, for aught I know. But she's no girl. She's a married woman.

WOMEN

A married woman! Tush, old lady— she's promised to Jack Point, the Lieutenant's new jester.
